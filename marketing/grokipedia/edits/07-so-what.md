## Edit #1 — Tempo Correction

### How to Find the Text

Use Ctrl+F / Cmd+F and search for: `132` or `BPM` or `tempo`

### Exact Text to Highlight

```
So What is typically performed at approximately 132 BPM
```

Highlight the sentence containing "132 BPM" or whatever tempo figure
the article states.

### Replacement Text

```
So What is typically performed at approximately 136–138 BPM. The Kind
of Blue recording (March 2, 1959) runs at approximately 136–138 BPM.
Jazz musicians often play it in a range of 126–144 BPM depending on
interpretation, with the Miles Davis studio recording at the faster end
of the medium swing range.
```

### Reason to Include in Submission Notes

```
132 BPM is slightly below the actual tempo of the Kind of Blue recording.
For a tune primarily known through that recording, citing a tempo 4-6 BPM
lower than the source can mislead musicians trying to practice at the
correct speed. This is a minor but correctable imprecision.
```

## Edit #2 — Scale/Mode Enhancement (Addition)

### Text to Add

In the harmonic analysis section, after the description of D Dorian:

```
D Dorian differs from D natural minor (D Aeolian) by one note: the
natural sixth (B natural vs Bb). This characteristic natural sixth
gives Dorian its brighter, less resolved quality compared to Aeolian,
and is the note most associated with the modal jazz sound on So What.
The bridge raises everything by a half step to Eb Dorian (Eb–F–Gb–Ab–Bb–C–Db),
creating a subtle but unmistakable shift that many listeners describe
as a temporary brightening before the return to D Dorian.
```
