### Replacement Text

```
The B section, or bridge, consists of a series of dominant seventh chords
following the circle of fourths (III7–VI7–II7–V7): D7 (two bars), G7
(two bars), C7 (two bars), F7 (two bars), prominently featuring the G7
chord (VI7) to heighten harmonic tension before returning to the A
section's tonic. In jazz performance practice, musicians commonly precede
each dominant with its related ii chord — Am7→D7, Dm7→G7, Gm7→C7,
Cm7→F7 — though this ii-V preparation is a performance convention rather
than a notated element of the original composition.
```
