Harmonic context: All Blues is a 12-bar blues in G major in 3/4 time, using I7 (G7), IV7 (C7), and V7 (D7) with a 4-bar introductory vamp on G7 before each chorus. The harmonic simplicity is by design — Davis wanted soloists to focus on melodic development over modal harmony rather than navigating rapid chord changes. The saxophones play parallel tritone voicings (Db/D) during the G7 sections, adding orchestral texture. Its waltz feel and modal approach distinguish it from conventional jazz blues.
