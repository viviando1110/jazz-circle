Harmonic context: Billie's Bounce is a 12-bar F blues with bebop-era enhancements. The basic I7 (F7)→IV7 (Bb7) movement is embellished with a quick change to Bb7 in bar 2, a dominant 7th (F7) through bars 3-4, and a ii-V turnaround (Cm7→F7 or Gm7→C7→F7) in bars 11-12. The harmonic rhythm is one chord per bar, keeping it accessible while the bebop vocabulary over the top is demanding. It remains the first jazz blues most students learn to improvise on.
