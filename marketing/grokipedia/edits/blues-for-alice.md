Harmonic context: Blues for Alice replaces the standard F blues changes with a chromatic descending ii-V sequence starting on Fmaj7: Fmaj7 | Em7b5 A7 | Dm7 G7 | Cm7 F7 | Bbmaj7 | Bbm7 Eb7 | Am7 D7 | Abm7 Db7 | Gm7 C7 | F (turnaround). This "cycle of ii-Vs" descends through all keys chromatically back to F — the opposite of the standard blues' simple I-IV-V. The form is a direct model of the "Bird blues" style and tests fluency in ii-V-I patterns across all 12 keys.
